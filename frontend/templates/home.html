Intelligent Environmental Health Monitoring System

A hybrid CNN + RAG platform that detects environmental risks from images and generates health-focused guidance, prevention measures, and follow-up answers.

It helps translate complex environmental signals into clear, practical public-health actions so individuals and communities can respond early, reduce exposure, and make safer day-to-day decisions.

Start Analysis

Environmental Condition Detection

The CNN analyzes uploaded images to detect environmental patterns such as air pollution, stagnant water zones, poor sanitation, and cleaner surroundings with confidence-aware classification.

Health Risk Prediction

Based on the detected condition, the system maps likely health-risk pathways and highlights possible disease associations to support earlier awareness and preventive decision-making.

Preventive Recommendations

RAG-powered retrieval generates actionable prevention strategies, hygiene and exposure-reduction steps, and public-health-aligned recommendations that users can apply in day-to-day environments.

How the CNN + RAG System Works

The CNN analyzes visual patterns in uploaded environmental images and predicts the environment class with confidence. Retrieval-Augmented Generation then grounds the health advisory in domain knowledge to provide diseases, preventive measures, and health guidelines tailored to that detected condition.

FAQ

What image types are supported?

The system supports common browser-upload formats including JPG, JPEG, and PNG. For best results, use clear images with good lighting and visible environmental context. Avoid very blurry, dark, or highly cropped images because they can reduce prediction quality.

Is this a medical diagnosis tool?

No, this platform is not a clinical diagnostic system and does not replace a doctor. It provides risk interpretation from environmental conditions and suggests preventive actions. If symptoms are serious or persistent, always consult a licensed healthcare professional.

Can I ask follow-up questions after analysis?

Yes, after analysis you can ask follow-up questions in the Report module. Responses are generated using the detected environment class plus retrieved health context. This helps you get more specific guidance on risks, prevention, and next practical steps.
